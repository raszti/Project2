Cheetah

A sprinting cheetah can reach 45 mph (72 km/h) within 2.5 seconds. The cat can sustain its top speed, up to 64 mph (103 km/h), only briefly.

  • Scientific Name: Acinonyx jubatus
  • Average Length: 3.5 to 4.5 feet (1.1 to 1.4 meters)
  • Average Lifspan: 14-15 years
  • Habitat: sub-Saharan Africa and in eastern and southern Africa

Cheetahs are big cats, members of the Felidae family and closely related to bobcats, lynxes and pumas. These graceful animals are identified by their unique black spots on gold or yellow coats and are known for their amazing speed.
